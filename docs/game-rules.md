# Game Rules

## Overview

You play as one of 16 contestants competing on a Survivor-style show. The goal is to survive tribal councils and avoid being voted out. In Phase 1, the game ends when only 3 players remain or you are eliminated.

---

## Starting the Game

- You are shown all 16 contestants and their stats
- Pick one to play as — this cannot be changed later
- Players are randomly split into 2 tribes of 8

---

## Each Round Has 3 Phases

### 1. Camp Life

- Your tribe hangs out at camp between challenges
- A few short story events play out involving you and your tribemates
- These events affect your **relationships** with other players
- Relationships are not shown as numbers — you only see the story events
- Relationships influence how others vote at Tribal Council

### 2. Challenge

- Both tribes compete in an immunity challenge
- The winner is determined by a random roll weighted by each player's **Challenge** stat
- Higher Challenge stat = better odds, but anyone can win on a given day
- The **winning tribe is safe** — no Tribal Council for them this round
- The **losing tribe** must go to Tribal Council

### 3. Tribal Council (losing tribe only)

- You must vote for one of your tribemates to eliminate
- You cannot vote for yourself
- Every other player on your tribe also votes
- AI players vote based on relationships and their Strategy stat
- Votes are read one at a time — each vote appears with a short pause
- The player with the most votes is **voted out** and leaves the game
- In a tie, a simplified re-vote happens among the tied players

---

## Stats Explained

| Stat | Range | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Challenge** | 1–10 | Contributes to your tribe's odds of winning immunity |
| **Social** | 1–10 | Affects how quickly relationships form or break |
| **Strategy** | 1–10 | Affects how smart your AI-controlled tribemates vote |

Your own Strategy stat does not help you in Phase 1 — you make all your own decisions.

---

## Relationships

- Every player has a hidden relationship score with every other player
- Scores start at a neutral value and shift based on camp events
- Higher relationship = less likely to vote for you
- Lower relationship = more likely to vote for you
- Your Social stat affects how fast your relationships change

---

## Winning and Losing

- If you are voted out, you get a **Game Over** screen showing your placement
- If you survive to the final 3, the game shows a placeholder end screen
- A full endgame (jury, final vote) will be added in a later phase

---

## What Is Not In the Game Yet

- Hidden immunity idols
- Tribe swaps
- The merge
- Final Tribal Council
- Any form of saving your progress
